# Compliance Guide — AzL Pools Outreach

## Direct Mail (Primary Channel — Recommended)

Direct mail using public mailing addresses is the safest outreach method.

- ✅ No consent required for postal mail in Florida
- ✅ No "Do Not Mail" registry exists in Florida
- ✅ Property owner mailing addresses are public record
- ⚠️ Avoid deceptive practices or misleading claims
- ⚠️ Clearly identify the sender and purpose

## Phone Outreach (Optional — Higher Compliance Burden)

If adding phone follow-ups:

- Check the National Do Not Call Registry before calling
- Comply with TCPA: no robocalls/auto-dialers without prior written consent
- Florida "Mini-TCPA" (eff. July 2021): additional restrictions on telemarketing
- Calling hours: 8 AM – 9 PM local time only
- Maintain internal Do Not Call list

## Email Outreach (Optional)

If using email:

- Comply with CAN-SPAM Act
- Include accurate "From" line and subject
- Mark as advertisement
- Include physical mailing address
- Provide clear opt-out mechanism
- Honor opt-outs within 10 business days

## Real Estate Licensing

If operating as a licensed real estate entity in Florida:

- All advertising must include licensee's full legal name
- Must include brokerage name and license number
- No misleading guarantees about property values

## Data Privacy

- Mailing addresses from county property appraisers = public record (no restriction)
- Phone/email from enrichment APIs (Melissa, TLOxp): ensure the provider's data is
  collected in compliance with applicable laws
- Do NOT use enriched data for FCRA-regulated purposes unless compliant
- Store PII securely; limit access; retain only as long as needed

## Recommended Approach

Start with **direct mail only** to minimize compliance risk. Include:

1. Personalized pool design rendering for the specific property
2. QR code linking to a web landing page with interactive 3D pool view
3. Clear sender identification and contact info
4. Optional: "Not interested? Let us know at [address]" for voluntary opt-out
